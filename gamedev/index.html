PORTFOLIO

Insignia

Insignia is an upcoming action adventure game by Uppon Hill. I am responsible for all of the audio design and implementation.

We use FMOD to integrate the audio into the game engine, Unity.

I also assist in the design and development of tooling to allow for an easier audio workflow for both myself and the game's composer, such as C# scripts extending FMOD's built in spatializer functionality to allow for greater control over distance attenuation for certain objects, and a system that lets us easily assign background music and ambience tracks to scenes.

Street Pool

A stylized first person implementation of pool that takes place on a city street. Made in Unity using the Universal Render Pipeline with custom shaders and lighting.

Wonderflower

Originally made as part of a two-week game jam, Wonderflower is a ~30 minute long game about a girl named Sherbet and her cat, Gelato, trying to plant a flower in a flower-less city. Features a cast of memorable characters, lighthearted dialogue, and a metroid-style map progression.
Made with Unity, coded in C#, with custom audio and visuals.

Abyss Demo

A short platformer made to showcase adaptive music & audio systems using Unity's built in audio. I also drew all the graphics and programmed the gameplay.
Here, I created a bunch of C# components to allow me to easily author spatial ambience sounds and slightly randomize sound events. There is also an adaptive music system that changes the arrangement based on game progress. Notice how the music becomes more full the higher the player climbs.

Penguin Surfing

A small infinite runner game where you play as a penguin surfing on the sea.

Made in Unity with handmade graphics and sound effects. It features a custom built transform component to allow for the slight visual parallaxing between objects closer to the camera vs objects further away.

Fish Synth

An educational toy/tool made with Unity used to teach young children about synthesis and music theory.
On the left you can drag around the squids to arrange notes in a sequence, and press play to hear it. On the right are various settings and rearrangable effects that change the synth's sound.
Any knob can be automated by dragging and dropping one of the 4 starfish onto it.

In addition to designing an interface that was intuitive and touch-based, it was important to make sure that the feedback from the interface was very tight; hence the squash and stretch and overall bounciness of each control as they are interacted with.
The sound engine is built using custom signal processing via Unity's OnFilterRead() function.

Crosley Singing Machines

An interactive piece made for the Trenton Free Public Library. Each machine was historically manufactured by the Crosley Machine Company. The machines can be turned on and off to create a small rhythm.
Made with Unity, all graphics drawn in Aseprite, and audio assembled in FL Studio 20.

WaveTracker

WaveTracker is an open source chiptune music production software that lets the user draw in up to 100 custom sound waves, and use them in up to 100 custom instrument presets. It also features 24 sound channels, a noise generator for drums, a multitude of effects, importing and editing audio samples, and a built-in song visualizer.

It is written in C# using the Monogame (XNA) framework.
